C.J. Wallace Excited About His Dad Notorious B.I.G Hall of Fame Induction

Anyone going through Hip Hop and rap music would not need more seconds to recognize the Notorious B.I.G's name. He is widely acknowledged for his songs like Juicy, Big Poppa, One More Chance, Who Shot Ya, Gimme The Loot, Suicidal Thoughts, and more. Many artists take Notorious B.I.G, also known as Biggie and Tupac, as the main inspiration of their career; after all, he has credited numerous accolades in the music industry.

Nevertheless, Biggie's years of hardships and contribution to the music industry paid off. Biggie, who died on 9 March 1997, will be inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame to commemorate his long-term music career. C.J. Wallace, Biggie's son, and an aspiring American actor spoke regarding his later father's musical excellence and the honor of getting such privilege.

Biggie is one of the 2020 class of honorees alongside several musical icons, including Whitney Houston, Depeche Mode, Nine Inch Nails, T-Rex, and the Doobie Brothers.

C.J. Wallace and his dad, Biggie (©: People)

Speaking with the show's host, Lawrence K. Jackson, Wallace mentioned:

"It's a huge year and for it to end this way — if he were here, he would be delighted. It's about time. He deserves it. He's an icon in his own and he deserves to be mentioned among other icons."

"People often forget that he was human. He was a regular guy. He loved to make people laugh. He loved to care for his people and do as much as he could for his friends and family. The Hip Hop Mount Rushmore — he is for sure on that list. He is this huge iconic figure and it's hard to separate the human from the icon sometimes," he adds "If the world doesn't know anything about Christopher Wallace, it's definitely that he had a good heart and he was a great spirit."

C.J. Wallace further said that his late dad would have been very excited about his Hall of Fame induction if he was alive at this moment. 'He would be proud to see how far hip hop, Black culture, and the inspiration of Black culture has come,' Wallace continued.

Furthermore, Wallace is very excited to find his father being inducted to the honor alongside Whitney Houston, whom he loves and adores from his heart.

How Much Is C.J. Wallace's Net Worth?

C.J. Wallace derives his net worth from his professional career as an actor and enriches a fortune of $20 million. As an actor, he made his debut role in the 2009's movie about his father entitled Notorious, where he portrayed his late dad's child character. The following year, he starred as Kenny Loftus in Everything Must Go in 2010.

Likewise, C.J. Wallace appeared in several other movies and tv shows, including Kicks (2016), Monsters and Men (2018), She's Missing (2019), Scream The TV Series (2019), Like Blood (2019), and many others.

More About C.J. Wallace

C.J. Wallace was born as Christopher George Latore Wallace Jr. on 29 October 1996 in New York City, New York, in the United States. He is the son of late rap legend Notorious B.I.G aka Christopher Wallace and singer Faith Evans, who raised him alongside her siblings, namely, T'yanna Wallace, Chyna, and Joshua Jahad Russaw.

Concerning his physical attributes, C.J. stands at a height of 5 feet and 8½ inches tall (1.74 meters).
