Gael Anderson Professional Career

She began her career in the film industry, but her primary focus has been working as a miscellaneous crew member in various productions.

Gael Anderson made her acting debut in the 1972 film ‘Gone Up North for a While,’ where she had a brief role.

Following that, she portrayed ‘Elizabeth’ in the 1975 drama film ‘Landfall’ and played a policewoman in the 1976 TV film ‘The God Boy,’ based on the novel by Ian Cross.

In 2001, Gael shifted her involvement in the entertainment industry to work as a floor runner for the comedy-drama series ‘Teachers,’ which aired on ‘Channel 4.’

Throughout her career, she has continued to contribute as a crew member in various other movies and TV series.

Parents, Marriage and Children

Gael Anderson naturally gravitated towards the entertainment world due to her family’s strong artistic background.

Gael Anderson Husband and Children

Gael Anderson happily married Andrew Lincoln, whom she first encountered while working as a teacher, serving tea to crew members.

Andrew, a guest director on one of the episodes, was immediately smitten by Gael, and they quickly became close friends.

Their love story continued to flourish as they welcomed two children into their lives.

Matilda Clutterbuck, their first child, arrived a year after their marriage, followed by their younger son, Arthur Clutterbuck, in 2010.
